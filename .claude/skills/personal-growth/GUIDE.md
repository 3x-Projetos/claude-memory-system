## Example: Applying the Framework

**Situation**: "I keep starting projects but not finishing them"

1. **Identify Pattern**: 5 unfinished projects in last 6 months. All stopped around 70% complete.

2. **Analyze Context**: Stopped when faced with tedious/detail work. Started new project when current one hit challenging phase.

3. **Form Hypothesis**: Boredom with detail work ‚Üí seek novelty ‚Üí abandon project.

4. **Design Experiment**: 
   - Choose ONE project to finish
   - Time-box detail work (max 2h/day)
   - Reward small milestones
   - No new projects until completion

5. **Track**: Daily log of progress, boredom levels, urges to start new things.

6. **Analyze**: Did strategy work? What helped most? What didn't?

7. **Iterate**: Refine approach for next project.

## Tips for Effective Use

**Do**:
- Start with observable behaviors, not intentions
- Focus on one area at a time
- Make experiments small and specific
- Track objectively (not "felt better" but "meditated 4/7 days")
- Give experiments enough time (minimum 2 weeks)

**Don't**:
- Try to change everything at once
- Rely on motivation alone
- Judge yourself harshly for setbacks
- Ignore context (what works varies by situation)
- Skip the tracking step
